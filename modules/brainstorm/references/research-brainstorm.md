# Research Brainstorm Reference

Use these questions to force a vague paper idea into a usable project context.

## Core

1. What is the concrete phenomenon, system, dataset, or failure case?
2. What does the current baseline or common explanation get wrong?
3. What is the smallest claim that would still be interesting if proven?
4. What evidence already exists?
5. What evidence is missing but feasible?
6. What must be explicitly ruled out to avoid overclaiming?
7. Which negative results should stay in the story?
8. What is the strongest reviewer objection?
9. What exact sentence should the paper make easier for a future reader to say?
10. What 2-4 word contribution name captures the idea?

## Paper Story Skeleton

- Problem: the reader-recognizable pain point.
- Diagnosis: the specific mismatch or missing supervision.
- Method: the smallest intervention that fixes the mismatch.
- Evidence: measurements that distinguish the diagnosis from alternatives.
- Boundary: what the method does not solve yet.
- Consequence: why this changes how the field should think or build.

## Artifact Checks

`project_context.md` is usable only if it contains:

- a one-sentence story
- 3-5 claims
- evidence per claim
- missing evidence per claim
- terminology decisions
- honesty constraints
- next unblock action
